Finding: across 521 stations and 35,040 fifteen-minute windows (a full year),
regional stress effectively never reaches the 0.8 alert line. Exactly one
window grazes it (max 0.8199) — an isolated, single-window blip with no
sustained event around it, almost certainly a sparse-reporting artifact rather
than a genuine regional overload. With the fixed 15 kWh capacity there are no
overload events to measure, so the overload regime must be constructed
(recalibrated capacity + coordinated demand) for the research question to be
answerable at all.
